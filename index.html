About

Summary

I am a postdoctoral researcher at Ruhr University Bochum working on kinetic plasma processes in the Earth’s magnetotail. My research uses large-scale 3D particle-in-cell (PIC) simulations and theory-guided diagnostics to study magnetic reconnection, dipolarization fronts, jet-driven instabilities, and energy conversion (e.g., J·E) across ion-to-electron scales.

I enjoy building reproducible analysis pipelines (Python/HDF5), producing publication-quality figures, and connecting simulation results to multi-mission observations (e.g., THEMIS, Cluster, MMS).

Current highlights

  • 3D PIC reconnection and magnetotail transients (DFs / DFBs / BBFs)
  • Energy conversion & partitioning (ions vs electrons; kinetic effects)
  • Front structuring via ballooning/interchange and drift instabilities
  • HPC-scale workflows: data reduction, diagnostics, visualization

Research

Representative themes (edit the text to match your current projects).

Magnetic reconnection in the magnetotail

3D kinetic reconnection, flux ropes, Hall physics, and multiscale structure of exhausts and fronts.

Dipolarization fronts and jet dynamics

Front formation, steepening, and corrugation; coupling to mesoscale flows and transient bundles.

Instabilities and turbulence at fronts

Ballooning/interchange, lower-hybrid drift, whistler activity, and their role in transport and mixing.

Energy conversion and dissipation proxies

Diagnostics based on J·E, pressure tensor work, and field–particle correlations (where applicable).
